# .impeccable.md — PlanMovies Design Context

## Design Context

### Users

Family and friends of the organizer (Jordan). ~25 people, ages 20-70. They receive a link in WhatsApp or iMessage. They open it on their phone, mid-conversation, probably while doing something else. They have zero context beyond "Jordan sent this about a movie." They need to understand what this is, want to go, and RSVP in under 30 seconds. After the first event, this becomes a platform for any group coordinator organizing any movie outing.

### Brand Personality

**Classified premiere.** Three words: official, cinematic, operational.

The clearance seal mark is not decoration. It's a signal that this is organized, someone is handling logistics, and you can trust it. The gold is a security-clearance aesthetic running through a film-premiere context. The dark background is not "dark mode." It's a theater before the lights go down.

The voice is a logistics coordinator: direct, specific, no theater. But the visual layer creates atmosphere that the voice deliberately doesn't. The tension between operational voice and cinematic surface is the brand.

### Aesthetic Direction

**Theme:** Dark. Derived from context: the user opens this on their phone, often at night or in a dim room. The film (Disclosure Day) is cold/blue/atmospheric. A theater is dark. The dark background serves all three.

**Color system (established, locked):**
- Three gold registers: hot (#c9a84c) for CTA only, warm (#b8984a) for title/badges, cool (#8a7d5a) for credit/wordmark
- Blue atmospheric counterweight (#2a3a5a) pulled from the Disclosure Day poster
- Near-black surface (#07080a) tinted slightly warm
- Gold is PlanMovies' identity. Blue makes this page feel like this film. Future events will swap the counterweight color, not the gold.

**Typography (established):**
- Inter for body text
- JetBrains Mono for labels, metadata, status indicators
- Barlow Condensed for pill labels and badges (OPENING NIGHT, RSVP OPEN)
- Fluid title sizing via clamp(). Shimmer animation on main title.

**References:** GMUNK's Tron: Legacy title sequences (constrained color on near-black). Film premiere microsites that create the feeling of the film rather than presenting information about it.

**Anti-references:**
- Fandango / AMC app (corporate, transactional, upsell-heavy)
- Eventbrite / Luma (template layout, could be any event)
- Partiful (bubbly, emoji-heavy, forgettable)
- Tech dashboards (metric-heavy monitoring software)
- News websites (the scrolling ticker was killed for this reason)

### Design Principles

1. **The poster commands the frame.** Every element on the landing page exists in relationship to the film poster. The poster is the anchor. Type, buttons, and metadata orbit it. Nothing competes with it.

2. **Create the feeling, don't present information.** The page should feel like walking into a theater lobby, not reading an event listing. Atmosphere over data. The RSVP form is the only transactional element.

3. **One animation earns focus. Multiple split it.** A single well-crafted entrance (poster fade-in) or state change is worth more than five ambient effects. Older phones in the family will punish excess.

4. **Show people, not abstractions.** "Jordan is going. Join them." beats a headcount ring or generic tagline. Family RSVPs to a person, not a page. Social proof is names, not numbers.

5. **Every element converts or it's in the way.** The page has one job: get someone from "Jordan sent a link" to "I RSVPed." Transportation fields, seating preferences, budget questions, reassurance text, chunky countdowns all tested as friction in ranger sessions.

### Constraints

- Single HTML file. No build tools. No framework. No bundler.
- All CSS inline in `<style>`. All JS inline in `<script>`.
- Google Fonts via CDN (Inter, JetBrains Mono, Barlow Condensed). No self-hosted fonts.
- Supabase via CDN for backend. Cloudflare Pages for hosting.
- Mobile-first. The link arrives via messaging app. Phone is the primary viewport.
- 11px minimum font size. WCAG AA contrast minimum. 44px minimum tap targets.
- prefers-reduced-motion must be respected on all animations.

### Validated Decisions (from Ranger Sessions)

These are closed. Don't re-open without new evidence.

- Gold stays. Blue is the film-specific counterweight. (GMUNK vs Ramirez, session visual-01)
- Reassurance line killed. Family context = trust is pre-established. (Murthy, session product-01)
- Scrolling ticker killed. Replaced with static banner. (3/5 voices, session product-02)
- Chunky countdown killed. Inline text instead. (Kim, session product-02)
- RSVP form: strip to 3 fields (name, status, party size). Transportation, seating, budget, showtime all killed. (5/5 voices, session product-01. Not yet implemented.)
