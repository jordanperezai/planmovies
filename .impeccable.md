# .impeccable.md — PlanMovies Design Context

## Design Context

### Users

Family and friends of the organizer (Jordan). ~25 people, ages 20-70. They receive a link in WhatsApp or iMessage. They open it on their phone, mid-conversation, probably while doing something else. They have zero context beyond "Jordan sent this about a movie." They need to understand what this is, want to go, and RSVP in under 30 seconds. After the first event, this becomes a platform for any group coordinator organizing any movie outing.

### Brand Personality

**Classified premiere.** Three words: official, cinematic, operational.

The mark is "the row": five rounded arches in a line, reading as both a row of cinema seats and a row of people sitting together. It carries the whole product in one shape — get the row together, your seat is saved, you're going with your people. The ember orange is the warm glow of the screen in a dark theater. The dark background is not "dark mode." It's a theater before the lights go down.

The voice is a logistics coordinator: direct, specific, no theater. But the visual layer creates atmosphere that the voice deliberately doesn't. The tension between operational voice and cinematic surface is the brand.

### Aesthetic Direction

**Theme:** Dark. Derived from context: the user opens this on their phone, often at night or in a dim room. The film (Disclosure Day) is cold/blue/atmospheric. A theater is dark. The dark background serves all three.

**Color system (established, locked 2026-05-29 — pivoted from gold to Ember Orange):**
- **Ember Orange (#D9480F) is PlanMovies' identity.** One warm accent on near-black. Shades: base `#d9480f`, warm/lit `#f1551f` (hover, the lit detail), dim `#a83710` (borders), bright `#ff6a33`. NOTE: in `index.html` these still live under the historical `--gold*` token names — the names are kept, the values are ember.
- Cream (`#f3f1ec`) — the "plan" half of the wordmark and primary light text on dark.
- Near-black surface (`#07080a`), tinted slightly warm.
- Blue atmospheric counterweight (`#2a3a5a`) from the Disclosure Day poster — an event-specific accent, NOT the brand. Future events swap the counterweight color, never the ember.
- a11y: ember clears AA on the near-black base (~4.66:1) but dips below AA for small text on card/elevated surfaces — use `--gold-warm`/`--gold-bright` for small text there.

**Typography (established, updated 2026-05-29):**
- **Poppins (800) for the `planmovies` wordmark — ALWAYS lowercase.** "plan" in cream + "movies" in ember. The wordmark is never uppercase and never set in another face. The 5-arch mark sits to its left.
- Inter for body text, buttons, form labels
- JetBrains Mono for DATA ONLY: countdown digits, prices, timestamps, phone/OTP inputs, seat numbers, share URLs. Never on labels or headings.
- Barlow Condensed as display font for headings, page titles, section headers, venue labels, badges
- Sentence case for section headers and buttons. Uppercase reserved for: page eyebrows, countdown labels, seat labels. NOT the brand/nav (the wordmark is lowercase). Nothing else.
- Letter-spacing: only on uppercase text (2-4px) and OTP input (12px). No spray on body text or labels.
- Fluid title sizing via clamp().

**References:** GMUNK's Tron: Legacy title sequences (constrained color on near-black). Film premiere microsites that create the feeling of the film rather than presenting information about it.

**Anti-references:**
- Fandango / AMC app (corporate, transactional, upsell-heavy)
- Eventbrite / Luma (template layout, could be any event)
- Tech dashboards (metric-heavy monitoring software)
- News websites (the scrolling ticker was killed for this reason)

**Learning references (not aesthetic copies, structural lessons):**
- Partiful: generous spacing, hero visual first, social proof as conversion, one-tap RSVP simplicity. Their aesthetic (bubbly, emoji, pastel) is still anti-PlanMovies, but their information hierarchy and breathing room are correct.

### Design Principles

1. **The poster commands the frame.** Every page opens with a visual anchor. On event pages, the movie poster/backdrop is the hero (full-width, dimmed, gradient fade). The movie's $200M marketing designs your page for free. On non-event pages, the visual anchor is the strongest image available (profile poster, crew photo, movie still). No page opens with text and a form.

2. **Create the feeling, don't present information.** The page should feel like walking into a theater lobby, not reading an event listing. Atmosphere over data. Forms are secondary to the experience.

3. **Breathing room over density.** 24px minimum between major sections. Content in cards with padding. The page should feel spacious, not packed. Vary spacing for rhythm. Tight groups for related items, generous gaps between zones. A page that breathes feels designed. A page that's packed feels generated.

4. **One animation earns focus. Multiple split it.** A single well-crafted entrance (poster fade-in) or state change is worth more than five ambient effects. Older phones in the family will punish excess.

5. **Show people, not abstractions.** "Jordan is going. Join them." beats a headcount ring or generic tagline. Family RSVPs to a person, not a page. Social proof is names and faces, centered, prominent.

6. **Every element converts or it's in the way.** The page has one job per context. Strip fields, defer complexity, use progressive disclosure for non-essential content.

7. **Social proof before commitment.** The user sees who's going before being asked to join. The crew list is the conversion mechanism. The form is the follow-through.

### Constraints

- Single HTML file. No build tools. No framework. No bundler.
- All CSS inline in `<style>`. All JS inline in `<script>`.
- Google Fonts via CDN (Inter, JetBrains Mono, Barlow Condensed). No self-hosted fonts.
- Supabase via CDN for backend. Cloudflare Pages for hosting.
- Mobile-first. The link arrives via messaging app. Phone is the primary viewport.
- 11px minimum font size. WCAG AA contrast minimum. 44px minimum tap targets.
- prefers-reduced-motion must be respected on all animations.

- Letter-spacing spray removed. Intentional tracking on uppercase and brand text only. (Critique pass 3, 71→25 declarations)
- Below-fold content: primer cards behind "Got questions?" toggle, activity feed with cap. (Critique pass 3)
