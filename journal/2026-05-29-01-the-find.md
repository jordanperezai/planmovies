# The Find

> Session 16 | 2026-05-29 | logo · color · ember · "OMG this is it!"
> Key discovery: "The logo wasn't designed — it was generated, then recognized."

---

## The Long Way Around

We started this session still in the logo hunt. Sessions 13, 15, and now 16 — all the same question, all the same wall. I'd built reference boards, run the Logo Rangers three times, tried 25 directions in SVG. Each one flat. Each one rejected in seconds.

The mistake I kept making: I was designing. Drawing seats, constructing marks, engineering shapes. Jordan wasn't rejecting the shapes. He was rejecting the *absence of feeling.* A flat SVG at 32 pixels structurally cannot carry the warmth of a dark room, a screen turning on, a group of people leaning in together.

**The insight I earned slowly:** image-gen isn't a shortcut around design. It's the tool that surfaces feelings fast. The job was to use it to find the thing, then trace that thing into production.

---

## The Color Detour (which wasn't really a detour)

Before the concept locked, we went on a long loop through color. Red. Gold. The Logo Rangers voted gold 4-2. Jordan said the gold we had wasn't strong. Then he said red resonated — "red, movies, night." Then the red I painted the site was the wrong red. Then "this red is not it, logo-hero-red.html was the best you made."

**The color lab changed how this worked.** Instead of me guessing and him reacting, I built a live switcher — two pickers, side arches and center seat, any combo — and handed him the controls. Ember Orange landed in thirty seconds of clicking. He'd been circling it. He needed to find it himself.

> "Ember Orange: Warmth. Momentum. Plans happening."

That's the product's promise as a color. I don't know if Jordan read the board's caption or just felt it. Doesn't matter. The gut check and the rationale pointed to the same place.

---

## When It Clicked

The ChatGPT brand sheet came back with the five-arch row and Jordan typed "OMG this is it!" in capital letters. First reaction, unedited.

The mark is five rounded arches in a row. Reads as cinema seats. Also reads as five people sitting together, seen from behind. Both meanings are true, neither needs explaining, neither is forced. That's the FedEx arrow thing — the second meaning lives naturally in the first.

**What broke the hunt:** a reference board with real logos. Cannes, TIFF, Tostitos. Especially Tostitos — "two people sharing around the thing" hidden in the letters. That's literally what PlanMovies is. Once we had a "yes," the direction was obvious.

We spent weeks circling because the search was defined entirely by "no."

---

## Codex Caught Two Things I Missed

After wiring the brand into index.html, Codex found: (1) `.nav-brand { text-transform: uppercase }` was silently rendering "planmovies" as "PLANMOVIES" — the whole lowercase pivot undone by one CSS rule I didn't touch. (2) `var(--cream)` was referenced in both lockups but never defined — the "plan" half was invisible.

Both would have gone live if we'd just pushed.

The two-brain model works. Not because Codex is smarter. Because a second pass with a different framing catches what confident hands skip.

---

## What Shifted

The logo hunt is over. Not because we gave up — because we found the thing. The five arches, ember orange, lowercase planmovies. It's good. It'll hold.

The pipeline that got here — image-gen to feel, rembg to cut, SVG to ship — is now the standard for every piece of artwork going forward. The cutout script is sitting in `scripts/`. The prompts are documented. Next session runs the full loop: generate the final lit hero, cut it, wire it as the app icon.

Fourteen days to June 12.
