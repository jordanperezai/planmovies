# The Critique

> Session 10 | 2026-05-27 | Design overhaul, features built, AI slop called out
> Key discovery: "The mono font wasn't design. It was avoidance."

---

## The Build

Nine features shipped in one session. Phone auth, OG image, activity feed, share prompt, countdown that always runs, status lines, photo uploads, a primer for skeptics, organizer manual-add. The technical debt from session 9's vision was paid in one pass.

The phone auth has an escape valve — "Skip for now" — because Carmen and Ray exist. You can't design a system that excludes the people it's supposed to include. The Twilio config still needs to happen in the Supabase dashboard. That's Jordan's task. Everything else is wired.

The OG image was one line. The old one was a portrait poster center-cropped in every messaging app. The TMDB backdrop ID took five seconds to find. A one-word change. Three weeks of bad link previews fixed.

---

## The Mirror

Jordan pulled up the Partiful screenshots and said it looked like a vibe-coded app. He was right.

The critique scores confirmed it. 20/40 on usability heuristics. 6.5/10 on AI slop. The automated scanner found 67 instances of monospace font, 76 letter-spacing declarations, 31 uppercase transforms. The page was a developer dashboard wearing a movie poster. The numbers didn't lie.

> "The mono font wasn't design. It was avoidance."

Putting JetBrains Mono on section headers doesn't make them feel cinematic. It makes them feel like a status page. The word "THE CREW" in 10px uppercase monospace with 3px letter-spacing and a bottom border is how you communicate "this was generated by an AI that learned to imitate premium design by reading Stripe docs."

---

## The Fix

Two passes. First pass: typography reset. Section headers, nav brand, eyebrows, stat labels, button text all switched from mono to Barlow Condensed or Inter. The landing title grew from `clamp(1.4rem, 5vw, 2rem)` to `clamp(2rem, 7vw, 3.2rem)`. The poster atmosphere — blurred at 6% behind every page — finally made the site feel like it existed inside the film instead of beside it.

Second pass: finished the cleanup. Inline styles. Video labels. "THE WITNESSES." "HOW WE GOT HERE." Every last one.

67 mono references became 13. Zero non-data mono anywhere.

Score after: 24/40 heuristics. 5.0/10 AI slop. Still not done. The 76 letter-spacing declarations are untouched. The uppercase count is 28. But the direction is clear and the foundation is different now.

---

## What Shifted

At the start of this session, the design looked like what an AI builds when told to make something "cinematic and premium." At the end, it looks like something that was designed for a specific movie, a specific family, a specific night.

The gap between those two things is Barlow Condensed and a 6% opacity poster. It turns out atmosphere isn't hard to create. It was just getting blocked by a font choice that made the whole thing read as code.
