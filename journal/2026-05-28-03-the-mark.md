# The Mark

> Session 13 | 2026-05-28 | logo · brand · audit · rangers
> Key discovery: "Clever and felt are different things. We kept making the clever one."

---

## The Audit That Wasn't the Story

The session started with an audit. Score: 15.5/20. Legitimate work — a broken token that resolved to nothing, a toast nobody could hear, a Supabase script blocking first paint, play buttons with no keyboard access. All fixed. Clean, verifiable, done.

But none of that is what the session was about.

---

## The Logo

The logo search started before the audit was even cold. Jordan kept coming back to a ChatGPT-generated reference: three seats, cream-gold-cream, stacked wordmark below, on black. **Clean. Cinematic. Already designed.** And every version I made kept failing to be that.

We ran the full exploration arc: seats, tickets, marquees, three-dot tropes, play-in-O, letterboard grids, chevrons, brackets. Twelve boards. Somewhere north of two hundred concepts. Two ranger sessions. A family panel.

The visual rangers split five ways. The verifier found a fatal flaw in the crowd-pleaser: Direction A's play cut was filled with near-black-on-near-black — invisible at 16px, and inverting to a visible black gash on a light chat-bubble background. Not a style problem. A technical one.

> "A small internal dark wedge on a solid shape reads as a defect before it reads as a symbol." — Jeff Johnson

The family panel delivered the other kill shot. Tía Rosa couldn't see the notch at all ("una manchita dorada"). Uncle Ray thought it was a chess rook. Marco: "says your spot, not our spot." Sofia: "sad-coded. A lonely chair in an empty theater."

The single seat means one person. The brand is "we're all seeing it together." The mark argued against the pitch.

---

## The Gap I Kept Making

After the seat rounds I tried type-led directions. Lowercase wordmark, play-in-O, three dots. Jordan said: "feels played out and done before."

Then the behavior-device round: the row that fills as people lock in, the lock-in bracket. Jordan: "clever but cold, doesn't resonate with the whole idea."

Both dead ends point at the same diagnosis. Every direction I drew was *clever*. The thing I kept missing is that "we're all seeing it together" is an *emotion*, not a system. The lights drop and the screen glows on your faces. That's not a row of rectangles. It can't be hand-coded in SVG rectangles and be felt.

**Codex tried. I asked it to use its Imagen skill. It told me it doesn't have one.** That was the honest moment of the session: the right tool for a felt brand mark isn't a code model. Jordan kept returning to the ChatGPT image because it has actual craft, rendered light, rounded forms with weight. I can prove things work at 16px. I can't make something feel warm.

---

## Where It Ended

`logos/logo-board-2.html`. Twelve more directions. The session closed before a winner landed.

That's not failure. Two hundred concepts ruled out is a map. The brief is now specific enough for image-gen to do real work: warm, cinematic, social, "we're all seeing it together," dark + gold, NOT nostalgic/precious/played-out/clever-but-cold. Every specific dead end makes the next prompt tighter.

The audit fixes are uncommitted. The logo is open. June 12 is fifteen days out.

---

## What Shifted

The logo exploration taught something that applies beyond this project. There's a difference between a mark that *identifies* a brand and one that *embodies* it. Most of what I made was the former: an icon that says "this is PlanMovies." What Jordan kept reaching for was the latter: something that makes you feel what PlanMovies is before you read a word.

That's a harder problem. And it's the right one.
