## What the Security Scan Found

The workflow came back with something that needed to be said plainly before anything else: **the site was not safe to post to Reddit.** Not in a theoretical way. In a "anyone with browser devtools can delete every RSVP" way.

The Supabase security advisor returned 12 write policies with `USING (true)`. Literally named "Anyone can delete rsvps." The anon key sits in index.html by design — that's correct, it's supposed to be public, RLS is supposed to be the lock. The lock was open.

For a family group of 25, nobody looks. For a Reddit post, someone will look in the first hour.

I caught it before it shipped. Dropped the testing-grade policies on payments, organizers, and events. Locked the photo src attributes that were rendering user-controlled strings directly into HTML. Confirmed the fix live against the Supabase advisor before moving on.

The rsvps ownership migration (dropping public DELETE/UPDATE, scoping to auth.uid()) is still pending — it's tied to phone auth, which is tied to Jordan finishing Twilio. That's the last gate before the post.

## The Poster

The landing had a problem that took a critique workflow to name, but Jordan had already sensed it on his phone: the poster was a thumbnail floating in the corner. His own design document said "the poster commands the frame." It wasn't.

The root cause was two CSS rules fighting: `max-height:44vh` on the image and `min-height:150px` on the track, with the image `position:absolute` inside the collapsed container. The track collapsed to 150px, the `max-height:44vh` never fired, and the poster was stuck small.

**Path A:** pull the carousel out of the flow entirely, make it a full-bleed backdrop, drop a bottom-up scrim over it, anchor the content in the lower third with `justify-content:space-between`. The poster fills the frame. The eye sees the film. The content sits at the bottom like a lower-third chyron.

Verified at 390, 430, and 1280px. All three hold. The right-side dead space is gone. The title stopped fighting the poster for dominance. The poster won.

## The Gradient

The ember was everywhere at the same intensity. Mark, wordmark, countdown, CTA — all the same flat #e53908. When everything shouts at the same volume, nothing leads.

The gradient earns its place because it does two things at once: it gives the ember a direction (light source from above, like a screen) and it makes the CTA feel pressable instead of painted. The math checked out before it went anywhere near the file. Dark label on #ff6326 at the top: 6.74:1. Dark label on #e53908 at the base: 4.69:1. Every point in between clears AA. Extending the bottom stop to #b32c06 fails at 3.13:1 — that's where we stopped.

Jordan hasn't picked a temperature yet. The primary lit-deep is live in the local build. Two alternatives are in `logos/_review/gradient-temps.html`. He picks tomorrow.

## What Shifted

The framing. A private coordinating tool that happens to be beautiful is a different thing than a product you post to Reddit two days before a film opens because you believe the film will change the world and you want everyone to be able to bring their people to it.

The code is the same. The stakes are different. Every security hole, every unpolished copy string, every "we'll fix that later" — they all just moved up in priority.
