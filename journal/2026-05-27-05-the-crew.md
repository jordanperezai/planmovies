# The Crew

> Session 9 | 2026-05-27 | Product vision, competitive research, the thing that matters
> Key discovery: "PlanMovies isn't events. It's crews."

---

## The Research Day

Zero commits. Zero deploys. The most important session yet.

We tore apart Partiful. Twenty-two screenshots, every screen in the app, the web version, the press quotes, the App Store listing. Then Atom Tickets. $178 million from Disney, Fox, and Lionsgate. Acquired by Fever last year. Social features stripped. The social movie ticketing platform that had everything going for it and ended up as a Fandango clone.

Disclosure Day is in their Coming Soon section right now. Same movie. Different product.

---

## What Partiful Gets Right

One question per screen. Social proof as conversion. Behavioral nudges over requirements. The event lifecycle. The photo that makes you feel like you joined something instead of filled out a form.

Partiful is good. The best, maybe, at what it does. But what it does is parties. The event is a date on a calendar. The content is a wallpaper and a description. The page dies after the party ends.

PlanMovies has something Partiful structurally cannot build: a content world that makes the case for showing up. The Movie tab doesn't just tell you when and where. It tells you why. That's the moat.

---

## The Named Crew

Somewhere in the middle of the brainstorm, the real product emerged.

Not events. Not movies. Crews. A crew that persists across events. "The Perez Family Movie Club: 7 movies, 93% Amazing." A page. A history. A watchlist of movies they still want to see. Stats: most reliable member, longest streak, favorite genre.

The movies change. The crew persists.

Jordan's reaction was the strongest I've seen in nine sessions. The Named Crew isn't a feature on a feature list. It's the answer to the question "what IS PlanMovies?" that we've been circling since session 1.

> Events die after the date. Crews live forever.

---

## One Movie

Jordan's idea. Pick one movie to represent you on your profile. The poster becomes your visual identity. Someone's profile shows the Interstellar poster and you know something about them before they've said a word.

The smallest feature on the list. Might be the most memorable.

---

## What Shifted

At the start of this session, PlanMovies was a polished single-event page that needed to be sent to family. At the end, it's a product with a soul. The soul is the crew. The crew page after five movie nights. Five posters in a row. Five photo albums. A watchlist. A history. People who belong to something that started with a link in a group chat.

The page still needs to be sent to family. That hasn't changed. What changed is what it becomes after.
