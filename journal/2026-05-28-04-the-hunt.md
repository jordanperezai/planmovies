# The Hunt

> Session 15 | 2026-05-28 | logo · brand · identity · exhaustion
> Key discovery: "The feeling lives in the world, not the mark."

---

## Twenty Directions

We started the session inside the logo search that had already consumed most of Session 13. Today we burned through the rest of it.

Tickets in every configuration. Abstract gestures. Image-gen renders (a glowing chat bubble with a crew inside, which briefly felt like something). The wordmark-as-logo pivot with Clash Display. The screen-in-the-O, which Jordan noticed himself and genuinely liked — for about ten minutes. Then the Logo Rangers, six voices, a clean 5–1 verdict for the screen-O. Then "it's dead, keep it for now."

Then twelve more concepts from the rangers in idea mode: The Count, The Gap, The Holler, The Roll Call, The Handoff, Showtime. All built. All rejected on sight. "Look horrible."

That's roughly twenty distinct directions across two sessions. Not twenty bad ideas. Twenty honest attempts.

---

## What the Hunt Proved

The thing I kept missing, and finally understand: Jordan isn't testing whether the mark is good. He's testing whether it makes him feel the thing the product is about. "We're all going. Get the row together. The moment before the lights drop." A 32px gold glyph structurally cannot pass that test. It never could.

The Chat bubble render that briefly moved him? It had rendered light, depth, warmth — it was a *photograph* of a feeling, not a diagram of one. The SVG boards I kept making were diagrams. Clean. Correct. Flat.

> "The logo doesn't look like a logo."

He said that on day one. He was right then and I kept not believing him fully enough to stop generating.

---

## The Logo Rangers

The team is the one thing that came out of this cleanly. Six real identity designers — Haviv, Scher, Hische, Draplin, Leader, Bierut — built from actual documented frameworks, not generic roles. Bierut read the files; the others reasoned independently. They converged on the screen-O on rigor, not feeling. Bierut owned the risk explicitly: "screen says *app*. Ticket says *event*. I'm picking the survivable one, not the resonant one."

That honesty is worth keeping. The session file is in `rangers/logo/sessions/logo-01`. The idea generation from session 02 is in `logo-02`. If a human designer ever works on this, that's the brief — two sessions of real deliberation, every dead end documented, the meta-conclusion earned.

---

## What Shipped

A clean wordmark placeholder. PLANMOVIES in Clash Display. Cream and gold. The O with a screen slot cut into it. Live on planmovies.com. And the mobile horizontal scroll fixed (it was `overflow-x:hidden` on `body` but not `html` — the scroll container on mobile).

Neither thing is the logo. Both are better than what was there.

---

## What Shifted

The logo is no longer a design problem. It's a sequencing problem. **Before June 12: ship the placeholder, build the world around it.** The poster. The dark landing. The countdown. The crew lighting up. That's where the feeling lives. A human designer with the brief can finish the glyph after the launch proves the product is worth the mark.

Twenty directions taught us what the question actually is. That's not nothing.

---

## What Shifted (the other thing)

The logo-maker skill now has a memory that will make the next designer — human or AI — start from a finished map instead of zero. Every dead end is documented. The reason each one died is documented. The meta-conclusion is explicit. Someone will read that and make something better in a fraction of the time.

That's what two days of searching is worth.
