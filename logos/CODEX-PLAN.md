# PlanMovies Logo Direction Plan

## Same Feel

- Dark app-tile first: near-black field, gold mark, cream wordmark. It should feel native to `logo-board.html`, not like a generic startup badge.
- Cinematic, but not nostalgic: no marquee/reel/poster-shop sentimentality. The mark should feel like the moment before the screening, not "movies" as a category.
- Social confirmation: the emotional center is "we're in / we got seats / we're going together."
- Bold figure-ground: one readable silhouette at small size, with any cuts using true transparency.

## Directions

### 1. Ticket As Object

- Concept: A single bold ticket stub as the core mark.
- Distinct: The ticket itself is the symbol, not a container for a check, play icon, seat, or initials.
- In-feel / in-territory: Board 4 and Board 5 prove ticket territory is central; the stub owns "admission" and "the plan became real."
- Risk: Can drift into generic event-ticket software. At 16px, notch detail and perforations may collapse unless the small glyph is brutally simplified.
- Effort: light

### 2. Confirmed Ticket

- Concept: Ticket plus check as "you're in."
- Distinct: This is the confirmation lane. It is about status, not group size, seating, or cinema.
- In-feel / in-territory: Directly grounded in the positively flagged ticket+check territory from Board 4/5 and the brief.
- Risk: Check can read as task-manager / payment app if over-emphasized. On white unfurls, the check must be a true cut, not a dark painted stroke.
- Effort: light

### 3. Paired Tickets

- Concept: Two tickets as "got them for us."
- Distinct: This is the togetherness-through-admission lane, separate from a single user's confirmation.
- In-feel / in-territory: Directly grounded in the positive two-tickets territory from Board 4/5 and the brief.
- Risk: Offset tickets can turn into a generic coupon stack. At 16px, two objects may merge into one blob unless overlap and angle are disciplined.
- Effort: light

### 4. Ticket Fan

- Concept: Three tickets fanned just enough to say "the crew."
- Distinct: Multiplicity is the concept. It answers "who's in?" rather than "am I confirmed?"
- In-feel / in-territory: Board 5 explored "we all got in" as multiples; this keeps the social meaning without adding chat bubbles or dots.
- Risk: More than two tickets can become visual noise. At favicon size it likely needs a one-ticket small glyph paired with the fuller fan at larger sizes.
- Effort: medium

### 5. The Two-Seat Bench

- Concept: A compact two-seat bench as "same row, not alone."
- Distinct: This is the seat lane. It owns proximity and shared viewing, not admission or payment.
- In-feel / in-territory: Directly grounded in Board 4's "two-seat survivor" and the brief's positive two-seat bench signal.
- Risk: Prior bench attempts risked couch readings. The silhouette must stay theater-seat enough without returning to scalloped backs or single-seat loneliness.
- Effort: medium

### 6. Reserved Row Plate

- Concept: A dark app tile with a simple gold row/seat-plate glyph, like a reserved block of seats.
- Distinct: The mark is not individual chairs. It is the claimed row as a social object.
- In-feel / in-territory: Keeps the seat center of gravity while avoiding the killed single-seat mark and cold "row filling" behavior diagrams.
- Risk: If it becomes brackets, progress bars, or fill states, it repeats the killed cold-device lane. At 16px it must read as seats, not a barcode.
- Effort: medium

### 7. Admit-One Stamp

- Concept: A stamped ticket form that feels like "admitted" without literal checkmark dominance.
- Distinct: It treats the ticket as a ritual artifact, not just an icon object.
- In-feel / in-territory: Still ticket-first, gold-on-black, and tied to the before-movie moment.
- Risk: Could become nostalgic or precious if distressed, retro, or overly print-like. At small size, stamp texture should disappear entirely.
- Effort: medium

### 8. The Stub You Keep

- Concept: A torn/kept ticket stub, simplified into a memorable asymmetric silhouette.
- Distinct: The object is the after-confirmation keepsake, not the full ticket, check, row, or pair.
- In-feel / in-territory: It stays in ticket territory while adding emotional memory without moving into abstract crescents or aperture rings.
- Risk: Torn geometry can look broken at 16px. Needs a clean small glyph and a fuller large mark, not ragged detail.
- Effort: medium

## Decision Process

1. Pick the emotional job before drawing: "I am confirmed," "we got tickets," "we are seated together," or "this is the kept stub." One job only.
2. Make one candidate per direction, not variants. Same colors, same app tile, same wordmark test, same sizes.
3. Test every candidate in four contexts: 16px favicon, 24px nav, 40px group-chat thumbnail, white unfurl chip.
4. Kill anything that fails recognition before discussing taste. The first question is "what is this?" not "do you like it?"
5. For the survivors, ask one question: "Which one makes you feel the group is actually going?" Pick the clearest answer.
6. Only after the mark wins, resolve Barlow Condensed vs Inter Black against that mark. Type should support the winner, not pick the winner.

## What The Last 13 Boards Reveal

- He does not want a clever symbol for movies. He wants a social proof object for the plan becoming real.
- The positive marks cluster around ticket, seats, confirmation, and togetherness. Inference: those are the brand territories, not parts to combine into one busy logo.
- He rejects loneliness, generic media tropes, and cold behavior diagrams quickly.
- He wants the `logo-board.html` mood: dark, gold, cinematic, compact, and serious enough to feel like an event.
- The next board should show distinct lanes, not incremental refinements. One direction should win by emotional job and small-size survival.
