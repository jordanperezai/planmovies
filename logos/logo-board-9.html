The system: small = your seat, big = the row

One mark can't span 16-64px. So: favicon/app-tile is the single solid seat (you). Everywhere bigger, it's the full row (you + your people). Shown in red — swap to gold if that's the pick.

Concept 2 (the pin) — parked. A location pin is the most generic mark in tech (Maps owns it); it says "where," not "who," and a seat inside it mushes at favicon size. The row wins.
My read. Concept: the row, decided. Color: red — it's the cinema seat, it's the only thing that's made you feel something across 20 boards, and it's the one that holds on a white chat bubble. Gold is prettier as "luxury" but it's been quietly losing this whole time.

Going red means pivoting the brand from gold to red — it repaints the nav, buttons, and accents across the whole site. Real change, worth it if the mark finally feels right. Say the word and I'll wire the red row as the full system and start the repaint. Or if gold still feels more "us," we keep the brand and ship the gold row.

Pick a color. That's the whole decision now.
