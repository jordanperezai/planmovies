Reference Logos — the "yes" anchors

Twenty boards thrashed because the search was 20 "no"s and zero "yes." These are the yes. Each one isolates ONE lesson worth stealing, sketched here in our palette — gold on near-black. These are principles, not marks to copy. Translate the lesson; never redraw the reference.

Tier 1 — One gold symbol on dark = prestige

Proof that a single restrained gold mark on near-black reads "event / this matters," not "startup / app." This is the tone PlanMovies wants.

essence
Cannes — Palme d'Or
single organic gold symbol
One palm frond has carried decades of prestige and scales 32px → 10ft.
For us: one confident organic gold shape beats cleverness. Restraint = prestige.
essence
Venice — Golden Lion
gold profile on black
Metallic gold silhouette on dark = heritage + premium, holds detail at any size.
For us: gold-on-dark is the event/luxury signal. A clean silhouette survives scale.
essence
Berlin — Golden Bear
minimalist gold silhouette
A simplified single-color animal/object silhouette is instantly ownable.
For us: if we ever do a symbol, it's ONE solid silhouette — no thin internal lines.
Tier 2 — The wordmark IS the logo

The only direction that survived 17 boards. These prove a bold wordmark can be the entire identity. (Set in Clash here as a stand-in for our live face.)

essence TIFF
TIFF — Toronto Film Festival
heavy custom wordmark, no symbol
Every letter is a design element. Maximum impact, zero icon.
For us: this is our live direction. Bar to clear: every letter intentional, not just "set in a bold font."
essence Sundance
Sundance
hand-lettered, organic warmth
Hand-crafted + slightly rough reads authentic, not corporate.
For us: "clean but dead" is the recurring rejection. Roughness/hand-feel can carry the feeling a clean geometric mark can't.

Jordan found the screen-in-the-O himself. That instinct is the right one — ONE hidden detail in a letter you already have, not a separate symbol. This is the canon for that move.

Tier 4 — Lives in a phone / group chat

Our mark arrives in a group thread. These are built for that surface — and they win on warmth, not polish.

Won Gen Z by being warm, fun, a little imperfect — NOT clean/corporate. Sparkle + life.
For us: a touch of sparkle/life beats precision. We go gold-cinematic where they go neon-party.

Tier 5 — Motion is the unlock

Memory's meta-conclusion: no STATIC 32px mark delivers the hit. Motion is the lever we never pulled. The static mark Jordan calls "dead" may be alive the instant it animates.

essence
Eventbrite — "The Path"
adaptable brush monogram, animates
An event brand's mark can be a verb — moving from discovery to the memory of it.
For us: the mark can move through the moment, not just sit there.
try this
Kinetic identity (general)
"the light turns on" reveal
Modern marks reveal via a sweep, a morph, a light turning on.
For us: the cheapest test we haven't run. Our screen-O literally turns on (dark slot → lit) on page load. Watch it loop, left.
Where this points next. The two unexplored high-value lanes:
  1. Tostitos move — a "get the row together" togetherness aha hidden inside the PLANMOVIES letters (not a bolted-on symbol).
  2. Screen-O in motion — the live mark Jordan called "dead" may be alive the instant it turns on. One animation, zero new concepts.
Warmth over polish runs through every tier (Sundance, Partiful, Airbnb): the rejection word is always "clean but dead." Hand-feel, a sparkle, a soft curve, or motion is what's been missing.
