# Reference Logos — the "yes" anchors

> The whole logo hunt thrashed because it was 20 "no"s with zero "yes." This file fixes that.
> Curated 2026-05-28 by research, on the PlanMovies brief: dark + gold, cinematic, social/event,
> warm, must survive at 16-24px and land in a group chat.
> Visual companion: `reference-board.html` (each principle sketched in our palette).
>
> These are NOT marks to copy. Each one isolates ONE lesson worth stealing. The job is to
> translate the lesson into PlanMovies, not to redraw the reference.

---

## Tier 1 — One gold symbol on dark carries prestige (cinematic)

These prove the thing PlanMovies wants: a single restrained gold mark on near-black reads
"event," "prestige," "this matters" — not "startup," not "app."

- **Cannes — Palme d'Or.** A single stylized gold palm frond. One well-crafted symbol has carried
  decades of prestige and scales from 32px to a 10-foot wall. *Lesson: one confident organic gold
  shape beats any amount of cleverness. Restraint reads as prestige.*
  Source: https://www.context.dev/blog/film-festival-logos
- **Venice — Golden Lion.** Metallic gold lion profile on dark blue/black. Heritage + premium.
  *Lesson: gold-on-dark is the "luxury/event" signal; the silhouette holds detail at any scale.*
- **Berlin — Golden Bear.** Minimalist gold bear silhouette (paired with a silver one for hierarchy).
  *Lesson: a simplified single-color animal/object silhouette is instantly ownable and small-safe.*

## Tier 2 — The wordmark IS the logo (validates our Clash Display direction)

The only direction that survived 17 boards was "PLANMOVIES" set as a film title card. These prove
a bold wordmark can be the whole identity.

- **TIFF.** A custom heavy sans-serif wordmark. No symbol. Every letter is a design element.
  *Lesson: a stripped-back wordmark delivers maximum impact when the type is doing real work. This
  is exactly our live direction — the bar to clear is "every letter intentional."*
  Source: https://www.context.dev/blog/film-festival-logos
- **Sundance.** Hand-lettered wordmark with brushstroke motifs, terracotta/olive/ochre.
  *Lesson: a hand-crafted, slightly-rough wordmark reads authentic and warm — the opposite of
  "clean but dead," which is Jordan's recurring rejection. Roughness can carry the feeling.*

## Tier 3 — One ownable detail in the letterforms (the FedEx "aha")

Jordan found the screen-in-the-O himself and liked it. That instinct is right — the move is ONE
hidden detail, not a separate symbol. These are the canon.

- **FedEx.** The hidden arrow between E and x. Invisible until you see it, then permanent.
  *Lesson: the best detail hides in the negative space of a letter you already have. Ours is the O.*
  Source: https://designworklife.com/negative-space-logos/
- **LG.** L + G inside a circle that resolves into a winking face.
  *Lesson: a friendly face/feeling hidden in a geometric mark = warmth at a glance, survives small.*
- **Tostitos.** The two middle t's become two people sharing a chip over a bowl.
  *Lesson: THE most on-brief reference. "Two people, together, around the thing." That is literally
  "get the row together." A togetherness detail hidden in the wordmark is our strongest unexplored lane.*

## Tier 4 — Lives in a phone / group chat (direct competitors + warmth)

Our mark arrives in a group thread. These are built for exactly that surface.

- **Partiful** (Studio Kaki). A "scintillating" sparkle mark, deliberately *not* too polished —
  fun, flexible, accessible, a little 90s. Custom shapes derived from the logomark, gradients, stickers.
  *Lesson: our closest competitor won Gen Z by being warm and a little imperfect, NOT clean/corporate.
  A touch of sparkle/life beats precision. (We go gold-cinematic where they go neon-party.)*
  Sources: https://studiokaki.co/partiful · https://brandfetch.com/partiful.com
- **Airbnb — the Bélo.** One soft symbol fusing person + place + heart. Stands for "belong."
  *Lesson: a single warm rounded mark can mean "your people / you belong here" without any literal
  object. This is the feeling we keep failing to get from seats and tickets.*

## Tier 5 — Motion is the unlock (feeling lives in the brand world, not a static glyph)

Memory's meta-conclusion: no STATIC 32px mark delivers the hit. Motion is the lever we never pulled.

- **Eventbrite — "The Path."** An adaptable brush-stroke monogram animating the journey from
  discovering an event to the memory of it.
  *Lesson: an event brand's mark can be a verb, not a noun — it moves through the moment.*
- **Kinetic identity (general principle).** Modern marks reveal via "the light turns on," a sweep,
  a morph. *Lesson: our screen-O could literally turn on (dark slot → lit gold) on page load. The
  static mark Jordan calls "dead" might be alive the instant it animates. Cheapest test we haven't run.*
  Source: https://mondaycreative.co/magazine/3-2-1-action-how-a-kinetic-identity-sets-a-brand-in-motion/

## How to use these on the next board

1. **Stop drawing new objects.** Tiers 2+3 say the answer is in the WORDMARK + one detail, not a symbol.
2. **The two unexplored high-value lanes:** (a) Tostitos-style *togetherness hidden in the letters*
   ("get the row together" as a real negative-space aha), and (b) the screen-O *in motion* (turns on).
3. **Warmth over polish** (Sundance, Partiful, Airbnb): the rejection word is always "clean but dead."
   Hand-feel, a sparkle, a soft curve, or motion is what's been missing — not another clean geometric mark.
4. Translate the lesson. Never redraw the reference.
