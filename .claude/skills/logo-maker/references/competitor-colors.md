# Competitor Color Landscape — where the white space is

> Researched 2026-05-28. The question: if PlanMovies commits to ONE color, which one is ownable?
> Broad competitor set (streamers + theater chains + ticketing + the Blockbuster nostalgia angle).
> Visual companion: `logos/color-territory.html`.

## The takeaways

1. **RED is the single most crowded color in the category.** Netflix, Cinemark, and AMC are all red.
   The color Jordan feels most (cinema seats) is also the one that makes the app look like everyone else.
   A red mark in a group chat reads "another streaming/ticketing app."
2. **GOLD is unclaimed.** No mainstream streamer or chain owns gold. And gold is the prestige-film signal
   — Cannes' Palme d'Or and Venice's Golden Lion are both gold. Gold says "event / premium / this matters,"
   which is the PlanMovies position. Strongest differentiation argument we have.
3. **There are two different reds.** Bright streaming red (`#E50914`) = Netflix. Deep velvet curtain red
   (`~#7a1f2a`, the actual color of theater seats and curtains) = "the movies" warmth WITHOUT the streaming-logo
   look. If red wins on feeling, velvet red is the differentiated version, not bright red.
4. **Blockbuster:** blue + orange, dated, two colors (fails the one-color rule). But its torn-ticket
   "Friday-night ritual" FEELING is the thing worth stealing — borrow the vibe, not the palette.

## Recommendation logic (for the chairman / next board)

- **Differentiation says gold.** It's the only ownable lane and it matches the prestige position.
- **Raw feeling says red.** But bright red = Netflix camouflage; velvet red is the honest compromise.
- **The synthesis to test:** keep gold as the brand color (ownable, premium, already live), and let RED live
  only as the single "you" seat / accent if needed — but Jordan wants one color, so the cleanest options are
  (A) gold mark, or (C) velvet-red mark. Bright red (B) is the weakest on differentiation despite feeling good.

Jordan decides by feeling — show all three lanes, name the differentiation cost of red, let him pick.
