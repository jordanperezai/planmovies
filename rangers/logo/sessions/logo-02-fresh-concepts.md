# Logo Rangers — Session 02: fresh concepts (idea mode)

> Date: 2026-05-28
> Brief: generate ORIGINAL logo concepts for PlanMovies, distinct from the screen-O placeholder and all dead directions. Warm + ownable + reducible. Each voice independent, no shared context.

## Synthesis

**Strongest signal: three voices independently converged on "the mark is a live count of who's coming," not a movie symbol.** (Bierut "The Row," Haviv "The Countdown," Scher "Roll Call.") Two independently proposed a negative-space "held/missing spot" (Leader "The Gap," echoed by Haviv's open-ring seat). Three proposed the "lights drop" dimming mood (Scher, Leader, Bierut). One went for the human gesture nobody else did (Draplin "The Holler").

### The four directions worth building
1. **The Gap (Leader)** — two gold people sitting; the negative space between them is the silhouette of a third, held-open spot. "Save my spot / the link is for the person not here yet." Most emotional + most on-brand. Risk: the missing-person needs a clear head-notch or it reads as just two people at 16px.
2. **The Count / The Row (Bierut + Haviv + Scher)** — the mark IS who's in, and it FILLS as friends RSVP. Forms: a row of dots that fill (Bierut), an open countdown ring that closes when the row is full (Haviv), or an "M" built from people-staves that light up (Scher). Every group-chat link renders a different mark = the share card is the logo. Deepest fit with the product. Risk: dots-in-a-row drifts toward UI/loading-spinner; needs people-cues.
3. **The Handoff (Hische)** — the "n" of Plan and the "M" of Movies share one stem, so the two words literally hold hands. Togetherness drawn into the seam where the words meet; keeps the wordmark, adds meaning. Risk: shared stem can blob at 16px; needs the n-shoulder open + M-apex sharp.
4. **The Holler (Draplin)** — a fat, tilted "I'm in" hand-raise. The warmth/life he keeps asking for; nothing else is a PERSON. Risk: hand marks skew volunteer-org/emoji unless kept weird-fat and tilted.

Honest read: **The Count** is the idea with the most product truth and built-in life (it grows as the crew commits) — and it gives the share card, the thing that actually recruits clicks. **The Gap** is the most emotionally precise single mark. Those two are the ones to draw.

## Full responses

### Sagi Haviv — mark & longevity
**1. THE COUNTDOWN ("Five")** — the leader-film countdown number redrawn as a friend-counter. A gold ring on near-black, stroke ~1/12 the diameter, a heavy numeral in mono inside. The move: the ring is NOT closed — a ~30° arc is missing at top-right, "the seat still open." In-app the numeral is live headcount and the gap fills as the last person joins, snapping closed when the row is full; static lockup freezes with the gap open. Fits: a countdown means anticipation, not nostalgia, and isn't on the kill list; it's a number about people. Risk: countdown leaders read insider; civilians may see a step-counter/notification badge — the gap must be obvious.
**2. THE TWO O'CLOCK ("Showtime")** — a clock set to showtime whose two hands are also two people leaning together. Circle, no ticks, two square-capped hands from a shared dot in a narrow V near the top: read once a clock near showtime, again two figures shoulder to shoulder. Fits: the link arrives with a time; clock = "be there," two-near-one = "together." Risk: clocks are crowded; the people-read may not land and it collapses to "just a clock."

### Paula Scher — wordmark as identity
**1. THE ROLL CALL** — the "M" in MOVIES rebuilt from four vertical staves (people shoulder to shoulder / a tally), outer two taller, joined at the baseline by a thin gold rule running the full underline of the word (the row/floor). Strokes animate in one-at-a-time as people join; at rest a confident logotype, in-app a live counter. Fits: "get the row together" done in the letters, not clip-art; the M is the only structural move so it stays a clean wordmark. Risk: a four-stroke M can read as Ш/a fence; needs a real type pass for favicon legibility.
**2. LIGHTS DROP** — the gold is the last light in the room. PLANMOVIES in caps raised one notch off #07080a (felt more than read); a single horizontal gold band of light crosses the lockup, lighting only the letters it passes; static = half-lit mid-word; in-app the band sweeps down and off, dimming letters as it leaves (the room going dark). App icon = the gold band on black, a horizon of light. Fits: "the moment before the lights drop" as light behavior, palette-as-environment. Risk: near-black-on-black fails in sun/bad screens; needs a high-contrast fallback; must survive as a still.

### Jessica Hische — letterform craft
**1. THE HANDOFF** — the final "n" of Plan and the "M" of Movies share a single vertical stem (the n's right leg becomes the M's left leg), with a slight inward lean and a shared apex like two arms gripping at the wrist. Reads "Plan hands off to Movies." Fits: the product is togetherness; the mark IS the join, in the one seam where the words meet. Risk at 16px: the shared stem reads as one fat vertical and loses the n — keep the n-shoulder open and the M-apex sharp.
**2. LIGHTS DOWN** — manufacture a descender: redraw the "i" of Movies as a custom glyph whose stem extends below the baseline into a long tapering tail hooking right to gather the word; replace the tittle with the gold dot (the last light on). The eye travels down the stroke like a room dimming. Fits: the pre-show hush as a letter move, no nostalgia. Risk: the tail can read as an underline/flourish and clutter; cap length ~1.5x x-height, keep it hairline-to-medium.

### Aaron Draplin — guts & life
**1. THE HOLLER** — a fat, cupped hand thrown up ("I'm IN"). One closed thick shape, mitten-simple: rounded palm, thumb hooked, three fingers fused into one paddle (no gaps to choke small), tilted ~15° eager; one negative wedge knocked out where thumb meets palm; optional motion-tick off the thumb. Stout slab wordmark beside it. Fits: the product IS the hand-raise ("Who's coming?" "ME"); reads at 32px as one mass; warm because it's a body saying yes; the tilt gives pulse. Risk: hand marks skew volunteer-org/emoji — keep it weird-fat and tilted, never centered.
**2. ALL IN** — three heavy stubby chevrons leaning in like shoulders in a huddle, stepping down in gold weight/opacity (more people falling in line), overlapping so the gaps are dark slivers. Fits: momentum, three becoming six becoming the row; never-finished feel. Risk: chevrons read fast-forward/media-player or startup-arrow — beat it with stubby warmth and uneven weighting. Gut pick: The Holler (nothing else is a person yet).

### Lindon Leader — negative space
**1. THE GAP** — two rounded head-and-shoulder gold silhouettes side by side; the negative space between them is the exact silhouette of a third person, a held-open spot. You don't draw the missing friend; the two who showed up draw them by leaving room. Fits: "save my spot / we're all going"; the product's whole job is the person not here yet — the mark has a space for you. Warm (bodies, not seats). Risk at 16px: the inner silhouette collapses to a plain gap; survives only with a clear head-notch. Aha is effortless once seen.
**2. THE DIM** — a full warm gold disc (the glowing house light) with one clean crescent of darkness biting the top edge (the lights dimming, the half-second hush). Two states in one: full glow + dark coming in. Fits: "the moment before the lights drop" as sensation, not object. Risk: at 16px reads as a generic moon/loading-spinner; more mood than puzzle. Pick: The Gap (the hidden person is the truest second meaning, and it is the product).

### Michael Bierut — system
**1. THE ROW** — five gold dots on a baseline: a row of friends / seats / a constellation. The dots ARE the data — each is a person; they fill gold one-by-one as people RSVP; empty = outline, full = solid line. The share card shows YOUR crew ("4 of 6 going" = four solid, two hollow) so every group-chat link is a different mark; favicon = one dot; app icon = full row on black; motion = dots light left-to-right like seats filling. Fits: PlanMovies isn't about movies, it's about who's coming; the mark counts people, the emotional engine of a group plan; warm because it grows as friends commit. Risk: dots-in-a-row ≈ loading spinner; push hard toward "people" (size variance/glow).
**2. LIGHTS DOWN** — a single gold point/bar with soft radial falloff into black (the last house light). The whole brand is a dimmer: app icon brightest, share cards dim edge-to-center, transitions dim-and-bloom, the showtime countdown literally dims the UI as the date nears. Fits: builds the system on the named feeling instead of depicting cinema; owns gold-on-black as behavior. Risk: "glow on black" is crowded/premium-tech-generic; the dim motion must be disciplined or it hurts mobile legibility. If forced: build THE ROW, borrow LIGHTS DOWN's dim-and-bloom as its motion.
