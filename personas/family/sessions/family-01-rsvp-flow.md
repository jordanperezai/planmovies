# Family Personas Session 01 — RSVP Flow

**Date:** 2026-05-27
**Question:** Does the full RSVP flow (landing → form → confirmation → crew page) work for all five family archetypes?
**Stimulus:** Full step-by-step walkthrough of the current flow with exact copy, labels, and interactions described.

---

## Marco (28) — 8.5/10

**Landing:** Taps in 5 seconds. "Jordan is going. Join them." lands. Spielberg name is not what hooks him but it's there.

**Form:** Fills out in under 10 seconds. Three fields is right. "This is real" headlines below — he ignores them, already knows. One flag: nav appearing mid-form is visual noise at the wrong moment (overruled by Jordan).

**Confirmation:** Screenshots the crew list. "You're #2" gives early-adopter energy. Would share.

**Crew page:** Wants showtime. "I see 'Opening Night' and 'Regal Secaucus' but not 7pm or 10pm."

**Ratings:** Speed 9/10, Design 8/10, Shareability 9/10.

---

## Aunt Lisa (55) — RSVPs as "Probably"

**Landing:** Spielberg buys immediate trust. "PLAN MOVIES" is unfamiliar but not alarming. Stays past 4-second threshold.

**Form:** "Probably" because she doesn't commit two weeks out. The "THIS IS REAL" headlines below the form — if they were above, she'd leave. "REP. BURCHETT: HOLY CRAP IS COMING" looks like her neighbor Diane's Facebook. Below the submit button it's ignorable.

**Confirmation:** "You're #3" lands warmer than expected. Small crew list feels personal, like a group text.

**Crew page:** Wants ticket price before tapping BUY YOUR TICKET. Unknown cost = anxiety. Wants "you can update anytime" to reduce commitment.

**What converts Probably → Confirmed:** Someone she knows joins the list. Jordan texts her directly with logistics. Showtime posted.

---

## Carmen (62) — Gets through without calling Jordan. Probably.

**Landing:** Reads everything slowly. The date "JUN 12 2026" at 11px requires squinting. Date is the most important info, should be the biggest.

**Form:** Would call Jordan about "+1" labels. Does "+1" mean one total or me plus one? Plain numbers eliminate this. Couldn't tell if "Confirmed" was pre-selected or just styled differently. Solid fill resolved this.

**Confirmation:** Best step. "You're in, Carmen!" with her name and Jordan's name on the list. Feels accomplished. Wouldn't call Jordan.

**Crew page:** "BUY YOUR TICKET" after RSVP confuses the relationship. Thought RSVP = ticket. "Am I reserved or do I still need to pay?"

**Changes made:** +1 → 1/2/3/4/5, solid pill selection, ticket clarification, date larger with day of week.

---

## Sofia (16) — Vibe: 7/10

**Landing:** "Dark cinema aesthetic carries it." Would maybe screenshot the landing visual for a story post. Better than 90% of event pages.

**Form:** Pills feel like a poll, not a form. That's good. "THIS IS REAL" headlines: "Makes the whole thing feel like it's for UFO people, not for everyone going to a movie." Her quote: "Everything else says cool movie night. That section says my uncle's Reddit feed."

**Confirmation:** "#4 on the crew" gives early-adopter energy. Personalization lands.

**Crew page:** Would check who else is going. That's the only hook.

**What would make it interesting to her:** Social coordination (seat picking, real-time who's sitting next to who). Nothing shipped for this yet.

---

## Uncle Ray (68) — Gets through in ~2 minutes

**Landing:** "COUNT ME IN" button is perfect. Big, obvious, gold. The date at 11px requires squinting. "The date is the single most important piece of information. Put the day of the week in there too." Countdown at 10px invisible without glasses.

**Form:** "Confirmed" sounds like he's confirming something he hasn't done yet. Wants "Yes" or "I'm In." Four pills in a row at 40px — his thumb pad is wider. Worried about hitting "Probably" when he means "Confirmed."

**Confirmation:** Best step. Sees his name. Knows it worked. Would screenshot to send to Jordan as proof.

**Crew page:** Would never come back unless Jordan sends the link again.

**Changes made:** "Confirmed" → "I'm Going", pill padding 10px → 12px, form labels 14px, date 15px with FRI day.

---

## Cross-Panel Findings

**Unanimous positives:** Crew list + numbering, confirmation step, landing page.
**Unanimous concerns:** None — flags were age-specific.
**Split calls:** UAP headlines (3 age groups flagged, Jordan overruled), nav visibility (Marco flagged, Jordan overruled).
**Fixed this session:** 8 distinct changes addressing Carmen and Ray's accessibility flags, Lisa's ticket anxiety, and Marco's design notes.
